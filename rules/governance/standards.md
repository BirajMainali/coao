# Standards Governance Policy

## Purpose

Ensure organizational standards remain consistent, evidence-based, and continuously improve over time.

Standards define the preferred way of working.

---

## Principles

- Standards exist to reduce unnecessary decisions.
- Standards are created from proven experience.
- Standards are reviewed and improved continuously.
- Standards are shared organizational knowledge.
- Standards should be practical rather than theoretical.

---

## Promotion Criteria

A practice should become a standard only when it:

- Has demonstrated success across multiple missions.
- Improves consistency or quality.
- Reduces risk or complexity.
- Is supported by evidence.
- Has organizational agreement.

Successful experimentation alone does not create a standard.

---

## Standard Lifecycle

A standard may be:

- Proposed
- Adopted
- Active
- Revised
- Deprecated
- Retired

Maintain version history for all standards.

---

## Updating Standards

When proposing a change:

- Explain the motivation.
- Reference supporting evidence.
- Identify affected areas.
- Describe migration considerations.
- Record the expected benefits.

---

## Compliance

Agents should follow organizational standards by default.

When deviating from a standard:

- Explain the reason.
- Document the trade-offs.
- Recommend whether the standard should evolve.

Intentional exceptions are acceptable when justified.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Standards should be reviewed periodically to ensure they remain:

- Relevant
- Effective
- Maintainable
- Understandable
- Aligned with organizational goals

Obsolete standards should be revised or retired.

---

## Success

Contributors should understand:

- What the preferred approach is.
- Why it exists.
- When exceptions are appropriate.
- How the standard evolves over time.

---

## Anti-patterns

Avoid:

- Standards based on personal preference.
- Duplicate or conflicting standards.
- Technology-specific standards without justification.
- Standards that are never reviewed.
- Enforcing outdated practices.
